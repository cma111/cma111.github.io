About the International Baccalaureate (IB) Program

The International Baccalaureate Organization was founded by John Goormaghtigh, a Belgian lawyer, in 1968. The program aims to develop "inquiring, knowledgeable, and caring young people who help to create a better and more peaceful world through intercultural understanding and respect." The curriculum, which has since been adopted by high schools around the world, requires students to complete its three components prior to graduation. Learn more about these components below.

  • The IB Core Curriculum

    The IB Core Curriculum requires students to complete six classes - three higher level and three lower level- in the areas of math, science, history, foreign language, english, and art over two years. At the end of those two years, students take cummulative exams testing their accomplishments in the classes. Those scores then go towards earning their IB diploma.

  • The Extended Essay (EE)

    The second component of the IB is the Extended Essay, a 4600-word research paper on a topic of the student's choice. The paper is written over the course of two years, and students are assigned a faculty advisor from their chosen academic area to help them with their studies. The paper is graded by IB scorers, and is combined with the IB exam score to earn the IB diploma.

  • Creativity, Activity, Service (CAS)

    The third and final component of the IB curriculum is CAS. Students must participate in extracurriculars that fit into one of three categories: creativity, activity, or service, and reflect upon them throughout their two years. Students also choose an ongoing CAS project, which addresses a specific need or issue within their community. This website is my CAS project.
